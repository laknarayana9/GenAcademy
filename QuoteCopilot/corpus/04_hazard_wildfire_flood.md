# Hazard Guidelines: Wildfire, Flood, and Claims (Synthetic)

> SYNTHETIC DATA. Fictional guidelines for demonstration only.

## Section 4.1 — Wildfire Bands (RC-601)

Each risk is assigned a wildfire band: low, moderate, high, or very_high, derived
from location signals. Risks in the high or very_high wildfire bands must be
referred under reason code RC-601 unless qualifying wildfire mitigation is present.

Qualifying mitigation includes defensible space clearance and hardened
construction features. When mitigation is documented, a high or very_high band
risk may proceed without the RC-601 referral but still receives a wildfire rating
surcharge.

## Section 4.2 — Wildfire Mitigation Follow-Up

When the wildfire band is high or very_high and mitigation status is unknown, the
system must request mitigation evidence as a contextual follow-up before
assessment. This is a missing-information pause specific to wildfire exposure.

## Section 4.3 — Flood Zones (RC-701)

Properties located in a special flood hazard area (FEMA zones A, AE, V, or VE)
must be referred under reason code RC-701. The HO3 form excludes flood; a
separate flood policy is required. Zones X and shaded-X are acceptable.

## Section 4.4 — Claims History (RC-501, RC-502)

Prior loss history is evaluated by count:

- Referral (RC-502): two or three prior claims must be referred for loss-history
  review.
- Knockout (RC-501): four or more prior claims exceed the maximum acceptable loss
  frequency and must be declined under reason code RC-501.

Zero or one prior claim does not trigger a claims-history rule.
